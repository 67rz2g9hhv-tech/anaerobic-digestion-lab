Step 1: Waste Input

Organic material enters the digester. Microorganisms begin breaking down complex molecules into simpler compounds.

🦠

Step 2: Microbial Breakdown

Bacteria work in four stages: hydrolysis, acidogenesis, acetogenesis, and methanogenesis. Each process produces different byproducts.

💨

Step 3: Biogas Production

Methane (50-80%) and carbon dioxide (20-50%) form as final products. Methane is captured as renewable energy.

🌱

Step 4: Digestate Removal

Nutrient-rich digestate exits the system. It becomes an excellent organic fertilizer for agriculture.
